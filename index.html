    • I grew up in a family of musicians, so there was never any doubt that I’d be playing an instrument once I was old enough. In 5th grade, I started playing trumpet. That was all fine and good, but I would really say my true passion was when I started playing bass in freshman year of high school. I did School Of Rock, which gave me the chance to experience live performance. It was there that I discovered how fun it is to be a part of the process of making a concert.

    I’ve had an interest in music production and technology for a while. I got into Daft Punk in elementary school, which in turn, caused me to seek out more electronic music artists. I became interested in how they made their music, and I got a novation launchpad for an 8th grade graduation gift. I messed around with it a lot, and tried my best to understand how to use Ableton. That same year, my parents signed me up for an audio engineering summer camp. I got to learn about how to use a full-size console, and basic techniques for recording and editing music. After that, I became enamored with recording technology. I got a small console, an audio interface, and a cheap mic. I would mess around for hours, not actually making anything, but just trying to learn the ins and outs of the devices I had. As I got closer and closer to deciding what to do for college, I realized that I wanted to be an audio engineer. I wanted to be the one behind the scenes, making the magic happen. It wasn’t until I actually got to college, that I realized I wanted to be a live audio engineer. I thrive off the energy of concerts, and I knew I would love to make them happen. I started working with the on-campus, student run sound company, Nola Sound. It’s a small gig, but I’ve been gaining more and more experience, and I hope to continue growing my skills, and eventually make my way through the professional world.
